Dr. Jacinto Convit

11 September 1913 – 12 May 2014

Jacinto Convit was a Venezuelan physician and scientist, known for developing a vaccine to prevent leprosy and his studies to treat cancer. He played a role in founding Venezuela's National Institute of Biomedicine and held many leprosy-related positions. Among Convit's many honors for his work on leprosy and tropical diseases was Spain's Prince of Asturias Award in the Scientific and Technical Research category and France's Legion of Honor. In 1988, Convit was nominated for a Nobel Prize in Medicine for his experimental anti-leprosy vaccine.

Key events in Dr. Jacinto Convit's life:

  • 1913 - Born in La Pastora, Libertador Municipality, Caracas
  • 1932 - Entered medical school at Central University of Venezuela (UCV)
  • 1938 - Earned his title as a Medical Science Doctor
  • 1940 - Began his teaching career
  • 1950 - Appointed as the Head of the Dermatology Clinic
  • 1968 - Elected as president of the International Leprosy Association (ILA)
  • 1972 - Founding member of the the National Institute of Dermatology
  • 1976 - Convit was elected Director of the Pan American Research
  • 1987 - Added killed Mycobacterium leprae to the BCG vaccine
  • 1988 - Nominated for the Nobel prize

Honours awarded:

  • Legion of Honor - France
  • Prince of Asturias Award - Spain
  • Nobel Prize Nomination
  • TWAS Prize
